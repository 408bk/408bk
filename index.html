RANDOM PRL POLISH NEWS

December 13th 1981

Imposition of the martial law

Martial law in Poland, 1981-1983 - a state of emergency imposed on December 13, 1981 throughout the entire Polish People's Republic, in violation of the Constitution of the Polish People's Republic. It was suspended on December 31, 1982, and lifted on July 22, 1983. During this period, a total of 10,131 activists associated with the Independent Self-Governing Trade Union "Solidarity" were interned, and over 70 people lost their lives, including 9 miners from the "Wujek" Coal Mine in Katowice during the suppression.
